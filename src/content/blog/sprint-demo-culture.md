Big reveals create drama, but they also create risk. A working demo every two weeks gives stakeholders a chance to correct direction while change is still cheap.

## Demo the workflow

A good demo is not a feature tour. It should walk through the real job the user needs to complete, including messy cases and handoffs.

## Make feedback concrete

When users see working software early, feedback becomes less abstract. They can react to language, timing, fields, and flow in ways that documents rarely reveal.
